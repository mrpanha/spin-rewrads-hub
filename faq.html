🏘️ Villages & Building
How many villages are there in Coin Master in 2026? +

As of 2026, there are over 570 villages in Coin Master. Moon Active regularly adds new villages through game updates, so this number grows over time. See our full Village List for every village name and build cost.

What is the best time to build a village? +

Always build during Village Mania — it gives either a 20% discount on all items or a 65% discount on the bottom-row items. If you can also time your village completion to happen during Village Master, you'll earn 2–5× the normal spin and coin reward on top of the savings. This combo is the most powerful strategy for efficient village progression.

Why do village costs keep increasing? +

Each village is more expensive than the last by design — it's how Coin Master scales difficulty. Costs start at a few million coins and grow exponentially, reaching hundreds of billions by Level 200+ and trillions at the highest levels. There's also a community-reported (though not officially confirmed) theory that your Star count affects your village costs — more Stars may mean higher prices.

What are Boom Villages and why do they matter? +

Boom Villages are specific village levels where your chance of getting rare and Gold Cards from chests is significantly higher than normal. When you arrive at a Boom Village, don't rush through it — open as many chests as you can afford to maximize rare card drops. Big Boom Villages are an even rarer tier with the highest drop rates in the game. Check our Village List to see all confirmed Boom Villages marked with 💥 and ⚡.

Should I build one item at a time or all at once? +

Always build all items in one session if possible. Building one item at a time leaves your village partially built overnight — and other players can attack and destroy individual items, forcing you to spend coins on repairs. If you complete all 5 village items in a single session, your coins are safe inside the buildings and you remove the repair risk entirely.

What happens after completing the last village? +

Once you've completed all available villages, there's no special end-game reward waiting for you. All you can do is stockpile coins and wait for Moon Active to add new villages in future updates. The game is designed to always keep adding content — so the end of the current village list is just a temporary pause.

🐾 Pets
What are the three pets in Coin Master? +

The three pets are Foxy (raid booster), Tiger (attack booster), and Rhino (village defender). Foxy unlocks at Village 4 automatically. Tiger unlocks by completing the Beasts card set. Rhino unlocks by completing the Creatures card set. See our full Pets Guide for detailed level tables and strategies.

What is the best pet in Coin Master? +

It depends on your playstyle. Tiger is widely considered the best for farming coins because attacks are more frequent than raids and Tiger's max bonus tops out at +417% at Level 300. Foxy is the best raid pet, doubling your raid coins at Level 100 and reaching +119% at max Level 400. Rhino is essential for protection while building villages — at max Level 200 he blocks 70% of attacks. All three are important and should be levelled up over time.

How long does a pet stay active after feeding? +

Each Pet Treat activates your pet for 4 hours of consecutive time. The timer runs continuously whether you're playing or not — you can't pause it by switching pets. You can stack multiple treats to extend the active period (e.g., 3 treats = 12 hours active). There's also a free 15-minute activation available once per day — look for the green food icon on the main slot screen.

Can I have more than one pet active at once? +

Only if you've reached Village Level 200+ and have the Pet Crew feature unlocked. Before that, only one pet can be active at a time. With Pet Crew, all three pets can be active simultaneously — giving you Foxy's raid bonus, Tiger's attack bonus, and Rhino's defense block all at once.

Does Rhino protect my village when I'm offline? +

Yes! As long as Rhino has an active Pet Treat, he will attempt to block attacks even while you're not playing. At max level, Rhino blocks 70% of incoming attacks. This makes feeding Rhino before going offline one of the best habits to protect your village progress — especially when you have unfinished buildings.

What are Pet Outfits and when do they unlock? +

Pet Outfits unlock at Village Level 210. They are cosmetic + functional items that boost your pet's performance beyond their base stats. There are four outfit sets (Charmed, Space, Viking, Ninja) with five rarity tiers. Foxy outfits boost raid %, Tiger outfits boost attack %, and Rhino outfits boost block %. Outfits are obtained from events, chests, and milestone rewards.

🃏 Cards & Trading
How do I trade cards in Coin Master? +

To trade a regular card: open your Card Collection → tap the set → tap the card → tap Send. You need at least 2 copies of a card to send one. Your account must be connected to Facebook to trade with friends. Gold Cards can only be traded during special Gold Card Trade events. See our full Card Trading Guide for step-by-step instructions.

Can I trade Gold Cards any time? +

No. Gold Cards can only be traded during special Gold Card Trade events. When one is active, the game notifies you and two specific Gold Cards become tradeable. Outside these events, the Send button is disabled for Gold Cards and you'll see the message: "This card is golden. It can't be sent to friends." Gold Trade events are frequent — check your game regularly for notifications.

What is a Joker Card and how do I use it? +

A Joker Card is a wildcard that lets you choose any single missing card and instantly add it to your collection — including rare, epic, legendary, and even Gold Cards. Joker Cards are obtained from tournament leaderboards, special events, Viking Quest, and the Golden Pass. Save them for the hardest-to-find cards blocking you from completing a high-reward set — don't waste them on cards you could reasonably get from chests or trading.

Which chest gives the best chance of getting rare cards? +

The Magical Chest gives the highest rare and Gold Card drop rates, and gives 8 cards per chest (vs 4 for Golden and 2 for Wooden). For maximum results, open Magical Chests at a confirmed Boom Village during a Cards Boom event. This three-way combination gives the best possible card drop rates in the entire game.

Why can't I send a card to a friend? +

There are two common reasons: (1) You only have one copy of that card — you need at least 2 to send one. (2) The card is a Gold Card and no Gold Card Trade event is currently active. Check your duplicate count on each card before attempting to trade, and watch for Gold Trade event notifications in-game.

How many cards are in each set? +

Most card sets contain 9 cards. Older or simpler sets may have fewer. Each card in a set has a different star rarity from 1-star (common) to 5-star (legendary) or Gold. You need all cards in a set to claim the full completion reward, which can range from a few hundred spins to tens of thousands depending on the set's average rarity.

What are seasonal cards and are they different from regular cards? +

Yes — seasonal cards are a completely separate collection from regular cards. They're only available during specific limited-time events (Christmas, Halloween, Easter, etc.) and have their own Albums with separate reward tracks. Completing a seasonal card Album can reward 50,000+ spins — the largest single reward in the game. When a seasonal event ends, those cards are no longer obtainable until the event returns (usually annually).

📅 Events
How often do events change in Coin Master? +

Most main events run for 2–3 days and rotate multiple times per week, with 3–5 events active simultaneously at any given time. Special events like Viking Quest and Seasonal events run less frequently but last longer (1–3 weeks). Moon Active manages the schedule dynamically — there is no fixed public calendar. Following dedicated Coin Master Facebook communities is the best way to stay updated.

What is the best event combination for building villages? +

The best combination is Village Mania + Village Master running simultaneously. Village Mania cuts your building costs by 20–65%, while Village Master multiplies your completion reward by 2–5×. Players who consistently time their builds to land during this stack progress 2–3× faster than those who build randomly. If Build Rush is also active at the same time, every building action earns event points on top of everything else.

What is the Cards Boom event? +

Cards Boom gives you 50% more cards per chest: Wooden → 3 cards (instead of 2), Golden → 6 (instead of 4), Magical → 12 (instead of 8). It's the single best time to open chests. Save your coins and hold off on buying chests until Cards Boom is active — you get significantly more value for the same coin spend.

Why can't I see some events other players have? +

Coin Master gates certain events behind village level requirements using a system called Milestone Master. Lower-level players may not have access to all events until they've reached specific village milestones. Moon Active also occasionally A/B tests events with subsets of players, meaning not everyone sees identical events at the same time. Check the Milestone Master feature in-game to see which events unlock at upcoming village levels.

Is there a fixed weekly event schedule? +

No — Moon Active does not publish a fixed event calendar in advance. Events are managed live by their team and can vary week to week. However, patterns exist: Village Mania and Village Master tend to run 2–3 times per week, Cards Boom appears roughly weekly, and Attack/Raid Madness rotate frequently. See our Events Calendar for a full guide to every event and the smartest weekly routine for using them.

🎮 General Gameplay
What is the main goal of Coin Master? +

The core objective is to build and complete villages by spending coins earned from the slot machine. Each village has 5 items to build and upgrade. Once a village is complete, you move to the next one — which is more expensive. Along the way you raid and attack other players' villages for coins, collect cards to complete sets, and use pets to maximize your earnings and protect your progress.

What is a Raid and how does it work? +

A Raid is triggered when you spin three pigs on the slot machine. You're taken to another player's village where you can dig up to 3 of 4 spots to steal their stored coins. With Foxy pet active, she digs the 4th spot for an extra bonus. Raids are one of the fastest ways to earn coins — especially during Raid Madness events when raid frequency increases and milestone prizes are available.

What is the difference between an Attack and a Raid? +

Raids target a random player's stored coins — you dig spots to steal their wealth. Attacks target a random player's village buildings — you destroy a building, which forces them to spend coins on repairs. Raids earn more coins directly, while attacks with Tiger active also earn a significant coin bonus. Both are important for different strategies.

What are shields in Coin Master? +

Shields protect your village from attacks. When a player attacks you and you have a shield active, the attack is absorbed and the shield is consumed — your building is not damaged. You can hold up to 5 shields at once, earned from spinning the slot machine. When shields run out, Rhino pet acts as a backup defense with a percentage chance to block additional attacks.

What is the Golden Pass in Coin Master? +

The Golden Pass is Coin Master's seasonal battle pass. It has a free track (available to everyone) and a paid premium track. By completing seasonal objectives, you unlock tier rewards including spins, coins, XP Potions, Pet Treats, and Joker Cards. Even the free track provides consistent daily rewards — the paid pass offers significantly better bonuses and is considered one of the best-value purchases for regular players.

Can I play Coin Master on PC? +

Coin Master is primarily a mobile game for iOS and Android. There is no official PC version. However, some players use Android emulators like BlueStacks or LDPlayer on their PC to play the mobile version on a larger screen. Note: using emulators is not officially supported by Moon Active, and doing so is at your own risk.

Who developed Coin Master? +

Coin Master was developed by Moon Active, a Tel Aviv, Israel-based mobile game company. The game has been downloaded over 300 million times worldwide and has been one of the top-grossing mobile games in multiple countries since 2019. Moon Active continues to actively update and expand the game with new villages, events, and features.
